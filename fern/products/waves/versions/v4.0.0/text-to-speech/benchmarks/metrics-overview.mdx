Lightning v3.1 TTS evaluations revolve around four pillars:

1. **Quality** — how natural, expressive, and well-delivered the speech sounds.
2. **Accuracy** — how faithfully the synthesized audio matches the input text.
3. **Latency & throughput** — how quickly audio arrives at the listener and how efficiently the model produces it.
4. **Aggregate quality scores (MOS)** — a single number that summarizes overall listener-rated quality.

The [Performance page](/waves/documentation/text-to-speech-lightning/benchmarks/performance) compares Lightning v3.1 (Standard) and Lightning v3.1 Pro against nine other production TTS systems on these pillars. This page defines what each metric means.

## Quality metrics — Naturalness category

### Overall (Naturalness)
- Holistic listener rating of how natural the voice sounds end-to-end across timbre, pitch, prosody, and pronunciation.
- Scored 1–5 by human listeners on the EmergentTTS test set.
- Use this as the single-number proxy when you only want one Naturalness signal.

### Naturalness
- How human-like the voice sounds, penalizing robotic, synthetic, or vocoder-buzz quality.
- Decoupled from intonation and pronunciation so a voice can be human-sounding but flat (high naturalness, low intonation).

### Intonation
- Whether pitch rises and falls appropriately for the sentence type (question, statement, exclamation, list).
- A monotone reading scores low even when timbre is natural; a melodically appropriate reading scores high.

### Prosody
- The broader umbrella of rhythm, stress, and melody — how well the voice "reads" the sentence as a human would.
- Captures both **what** the model emphasizes (which word in the sentence gets the stress) and **when** it pauses.

### Pronunciation (listener-rated)
- Whether individual words are phonetically correct, especially proper nouns, loanwords, technical terms, and domain-specific vocabulary.
- Listener-rated on a 1–5 scale; distinct from the Whisper-judged Pronunciation % under Accuracy below.

### Audio Quality
- Technical cleanliness of the output: absence of artifacts, distortion, clipping, popping, background hiss, or vocoder warble.

## Quality metrics — Expressiveness category

### Overall (Expressiveness)
- Holistic listener rating of how expressive the voice sounds given the sentence's context.
- A neutral declarative gets a different "right answer" than an exclamation or a question; this metric judges fit, not max-expressiveness.

### Paralinguistics
- Non-verbal vocal elements — laughter, sighs, gasps, filler sounds ("um", "uh", "hmm") — and whether they're rendered with realistic timing and amplitude.

### Emotions
- How accurately the voice conveys the intended emotional tone: neutral, warm, urgent, apologetic, excited, somber.
- Measured on emotionally-loaded test sentences from the EmergentTTS benchmark.

## Quality metrics — Delivery category

### Boundary Consistency
- Whether phrase and sentence boundaries are marked consistently with pauses or pitch shifts, without arbitrary breaks mid-phrase.

### Pronunciation Style
- Not just phonetic correctness but stylistic choices: formal vs. casual register, regional accent consistency, honorific handling, recognition of named entities as units rather than letter-by-letter spell-outs.

### Natural Pace
- Whether the speaking rate feels comfortable and appropriate for the content type — neither rushed nor dragging.

### Pause Placement
- Whether silences appear at semantically correct points (after commas, between clauses, before quoted speech) rather than mid-word or mid-phrase.

### Breathing Naturalness
- Whether breath sounds occur at realistic points and with realistic frequency — not absent entirely, not inserted randomly, not in the middle of a word.

## Accuracy metrics

### Word Error Rate (WER)
- `WER = (Substitutions + Deletions + Insertions) ÷ Total Words`.
- Measures how faithfully the TTS renders the input text. The TTS audio is decoded with Whisper and compared back to the original input.
- **Lower is better.** Direction matters because input → audio → input round-trip is what we're scoring.

### Character Error Rate (CER)
- Like WER but at the character level. Useful for languages with compact scripts or heavy compounding.
- **Lower is better.**

### Hallucination
- Words or sounds the TTS generates that have no basis in the input text — insertions, substitutions, or fabricated content.
- **Lower is better.**

### Deletion
- Words from the input text that the TTS dropped entirely.
- **Lower is better.**

### Pronunciation %
- The proportion of words pronounced correctly out of total words (Whisper recoverable from the audio).
- **Higher is better.**

### Whisper jiwer vs Whisper LLM
- Two scoring methodologies for the same metric family:
  - **Whisper jiwer** — raw Whisper-decoded transcripts compared to input via the `jiwer` library. Penalizes any character difference.
  - **Whisper LLM** — Whisper transcripts post-processed by an LLM to normalize punctuation, casing, and Whisper's own transcription noise. Tends to give lower error rates by reducing false positives.
- Both methodologies are reported on the [Performance page](/waves/documentation/text-to-speech-lightning/benchmarks/performance) where available.

## Latency & throughput metrics

### Time-to-First-Byte (TTFB)
- Wall-clock delay between sending the synthesis request and the first audio byte arriving.
- For real-time agents, target **< 250 ms** to maintain natural turn-taking.
- Lightning v3.1 (both Standard and Pro) hits ~200 ms TTFB at 40 concurrent WebSocket connections.

### End-to-end latency
- Total wall-clock time from request submission to the last audio byte arriving.
- For sync HTTP, this equals "time to complete clip"; for streaming, it's set by the playback rate.
- Report p50/p90/p95 to capture outliers from long inputs.

### Real-Time Factor (RTF)
- `RTF = Audio Duration ÷ Processing Time`.
- Values above 1.0 mean the model produces audio faster than playback (i.e., it can sustain real-time streaming).
- Lightning v3.1 runs at **3.3× RTF** on NVIDIA L40S.

### Concurrency
- Number of simultaneous synthesis requests a single deployment can sustain while meeting the published TTFB/RTF targets.
- Quoted at 40 concurrent requests for the Lightning v3.1 production deployment.

## Aggregate quality scores (MOS v2)

### Mean MOS
- **M**ean **O**pinion **S**core: average listener rating on a 1–5 scale across the test set.
- The canonical aggregate quality metric in TTS literature.
- **Higher is better.**

### UTMOS
- A predicted MOS from the UTMOS reference model — an automated proxy for subjective quality.
- Useful for CI gating where human evaluation is too slow or expensive.
- **Higher is better.**

### WV-MOS
- A predicted MOS from the WavLM-based WV-MOS reference model.
- Reported alongside UTMOS for cross-validation; predictive MOS models can disagree on edge cases so reporting both reduces noise.
- **Higher is better.**

## What's not in the published numbers

The Performance page reports aggregate results across the full 1,088-sample EmergentTTS test set. It does **not** report:

- **Per-voice** breakdowns. Voice-level scores vary because the listener-rated dimensions (Naturalness, Intonation, etc.) are partly a function of the specific voice's timbre and style.
- **Per-language** breakdowns. EmergentTTS is predominantly English with multilingual code-switching subsets; for evaluating a specific non-English language, run the [Evaluation Walkthrough](/waves/documentation/text-to-speech-lightning/benchmarks/evaluation-walkthrough) on your own dataset.
- **Output format / sample rate** breakdowns. All scores assume the model's native 44.1 kHz output. Lower sample rates (24 kHz, 16 kHz, 8 kHz) and lossy encodings (`mp3`, `mulaw`, `alaw`) introduce additional quality loss not reflected in these numbers.
