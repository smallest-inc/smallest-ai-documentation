This page lists the recommended cloud instance types for self-hosting Pulse and Pulse Pro. The recommendations are based on the GPUs we benchmark against internally; the broader [hardware requirements](./prerequisites/hardware-requirements) page lists every supported GPU class.

<Warning>
Pricing varies by region, commitment term (on-demand, 1-year reserved, 3-year reserved, spot), and quota availability. Confirm rate and quota in your account before sizing a deployment.
</Warning>

## AWS

| Model | Instance type | GPU | vCPU | RAM | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Pulse Pro** | [`g6e.xlarge`](https://aws.amazon.com/ec2/instance-types/g6e/) | 1× NVIDIA L40S (48 GB) | 4 | 32 GB | Reference benchmark hardware. On-demand list price ~$1.86/hr in `us-east-1`. |
| Pulse Pro (alt) | [`p5.48xlarge`](https://aws.amazon.com/ec2/instance-types/p5/) | 8× H100 | 192 | 2 TB | Multi-tenant or very high RPS. Overkill for a single worker. |
| **Pulse** | [`g6.xlarge`](https://aws.amazon.com/ec2/instance-types/g6/) | 1× NVIDIA L4 (24 GB) | 4 | 16 GB | Production reference for Pulse multilingual. |
| Pulse (budget) | [`g4dn.xlarge`](https://aws.amazon.com/ec2/instance-types/g4/) | 1× NVIDIA T4 (16 GB) | 4 | 16 GB | Older T4 still supported; reduced throughput. |

Region availability: L40S and L4 instances are available in major regions (`us-east-1`, `us-west-2`, `eu-west-1`, `ap-south-1`). Check the [EC2 instance availability matrix](https://aws.amazon.com/ec2/pricing/on-demand/) for your target region before deployment.

## Picking between on-demand, reserved, and spot

- **On-demand** for proof-of-concept and bursty workloads.
- **1-year or 3-year reserved** for steady-state production. Materially reduces hourly rate (commonly 30–60% vs on-demand).
- **Spot / preemptible** for batch / overnight transcription. Cheapest option, but the worker can be preempted; build your queue to tolerate restarts.

## Container image

Once you have the GPU host, follow the [Quick Start](./quick-start) to pull the Pulse / Pulse Pro container image and run the worker. The image runs identically across AWS, GCP, and Azure; the only difference is the GPU driver version on the host.
