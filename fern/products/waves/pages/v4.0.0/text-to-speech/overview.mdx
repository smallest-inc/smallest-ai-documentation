The Lightning TTS API converts text into natural speech via `https://api.smallest.ai/waves/v1`. 29 languages across two pools — the standard 217-voice Lightning v3.1 catalog (12 languages) and the premium Lightning v3.1 Pro pool (all 29) — at 44.1 kHz native sample rate, ~200ms TTFB, with sync, SSE, and WebSocket streaming.

**Hear Lightning v3.1 Pro** (voice: `meher`, model: `lightning_v3.1_pro`):

## Synthesis Modes

Choose the synthesis mode that best fits your application's needs:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Synchronous" icon="file-audio" href="/waves/documentation/text-to-speech-lightning/sync-async">
    Generate complete audio files with a single HTTP request. Ideal for pre-rendering content, batch processing, and applications where immediate streaming isn't required.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Streaming" icon="broadcast-tower" href="/waves/documentation/text-to-speech-lightning/streaming">
    Receive audio chunks as they're generated via WebSocket. Perfect for real-time voice assistants, live narration, and low-latency conversational AI.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Feature Highlights

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Ultra-Low Latency">
    Optimized streaming pipeline delivers ~200ms time-to-first-byte (TTFB) for real-time applications. Lightning v3.1 achieves even faster response times for conversational AI.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Voice Cloning">
    Create custom voice profiles by uploading audio samples. Instant voice cloning works with just a few seconds of audio, while professional voice cloning delivers studio-quality results.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="29 Languages">
    Lightning v3.1 covers 12 languages — English, Hindi, Spanish, and 9 Indian languages (Marathi, Kannada, Tamil, Bengali, Gujarati, Telugu, Malayalam, Punjabi, Odia). Lightning v3.1 Pro adds 27 additional Pro languages (9 Indian, 8 Asian & Middle Eastern, 10 European) — 29 unique languages across the two models. See the [Lightning v3.1](/waves/model-cards/text-to-speech/lightning-v-3-1#supported-languages) and [Lightning v3.1 Pro](/waves/model-cards/text-to-speech/lightning-v-3-1-pro#additional-pro-languages) model cards for per-language voice counts.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Multiple Output Formats">
    Choose from PCM, WAV, MP3, or μ-law encoding. Configurable sample rates from 8kHz to 44kHz to match your application's requirements.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Speed Control">
    Adjust speech rate with a simple multiplier. Slow down for clarity or speed up for faster content delivery without pitch distortion.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pronunciation Dictionaries">
    Define custom pronunciations for brand names, technical terms, and acronyms. Ensure consistent, accurate pronunciation across all synthesized audio.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="High-Quality Audio">
    Lightning v3.1 produces 44 kHz audio with natural prosody and expressiveness. Perfect for audiobooks, podcasts, and premium voice experiences.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="WebSocket Streaming">
    Persistent connections for continuous audio streaming. Ideal for voice bots and interactive applications where latency is critical.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
**Pro language support is per voice.** Indian Pro voices (e.g., `meher`, `rhea`, `aviraj`) speak English with native Hindi code-switching. British and American Pro voices speak English only. Each additional Pro language has its own dedicated voices — pass the matching ISO 639-1 `language` code with a voice from that language (see the [Pro voice catalog](/waves/model-cards/text-to-speech/lightning-v-3-1-pro#voice-catalog)). For languages without Pro voices, use standard Lightning v3.1.
</Note>
